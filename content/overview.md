### Your MP - John Milne (Liberal Democrat)

The Horsham parliamentary constituency is represented in the UK Parliament by John Milne, a member of the Liberal Democrats, who won the seat in the 2024 general election—marking the **first time since 1876** that a non-Conservative MP has held the seat.

John is a member of the Liberal Democrats and the party's spokesperson for the environment, energy and climate change.

He is also the party's spokesperson for the 

    - economy, business and trade.
    - transport, infrastructure and housing.
    - education, skills and employment.
    - health and social care.
    - justice and home affairs.
    - foreign and commonwealth affairs.
    - defence and international security.

### Horsham District Council

At the local level, Horsham District Council is composed of elected councillors who represent specific wards within the district.  The council includes representatives from various political parties, including the Liberal Democrats, Green Party, Conservatives, and others. For example:

    - Councillor **Martin Boffey** – Leader of the Council (Liberal Democrats)
    - Councillor **Tony Bevis** – Chairman of the Council (Liberal Democrats)

<br> 


### West Sussex County Council

Councillors of West Sussex County Council are 70 elected representatives, each serving one of 70 electoral divisions across the county, with each division representing approximately 12,000 residents. 

As of January 2026, the political composition is:

    - Conservative: 40 councillors
    - Liberal Democrat: 10
    - Labour: 9
    - Reform UK: 4
    - Green and Independent Alliance: 3 (comprising 2 Independents and 1 Green councillor)
    - Independent: 2
    - Vacancy: 1
